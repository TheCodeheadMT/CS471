What is Bayes' Rule?

Bayes' Theorem calculates the probability of an event based on prior knowledge of conditions related to the event. Instead of guessing blindly, it answers: Given this new evidence, what is the actual probability my hypothesis is true?

$$P(H|E) = \frac{P(E|H) \cdot P(H)}{P(E)}$$
P(H|E) [Posterior]: Probability hypothesis $H$ is true after seeing evidence $E$.
P(E|H) [Likelihood]: Probability evidence $E$ appears if hypothesis is true.
P(H) [Prior]: Your initial belief before seeing any new evidence.
